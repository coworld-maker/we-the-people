# Competitive Analysis — Democracy Unlocked

**Date:** June 12, 2026
**Author:** Product (with council review)
**Purpose:** Map the civic-engagement / bill-tracking landscape, identify where Democracy Unlocked is differentiated vs. commoditized, and inform positioning.

---

## 1. The landscape, by overlap with our core loop

Our core loop: **find a bill → read a plain-English AI summary → vote on it as a citizen → see how your rep voted → see who's lobbying/funding it → discuss → contact your rep.**

### Tier A — Near-clones (direct threat, all recent, all "free forever")

| App | AI summaries | Citizen voting | Rep voting records | Contact reps | Discussions | Lobbying/donor | Notable edge |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **CivicAlign** | ✅ + citations | ❌ | ✅ | ~ implied | ❌ | FEC (candidates) | Alignment scoring, 2026 candidate comparisons, strict non-partisan |
| **BillBoard** (mybillboard.org) | ✅ | ❌ | ✅ | ✅ one-tap templated/AI email | ❌ | ❌ | "Personal impact" framing; passage-probability |
| **How Congress Votes** | ✅ + Q&A chatbot | ❌ | ✅ | ~ | ❌ | ❌ | Conversational bill Q&A |
| **Better Congress** | ✅ on-demand | ❌ | ~ | ~ | ❌ | ❌ | Real-time plain-English explainers |
| **Democracy Unlocked (us)** | ✅ | ✅ | ✅ | ✅ (template, not auto-send) | ✅ | ✅ LDA + FEC tied to bill | Community + accountability |

### Tier B — Established incumbents (bigger or older, narrower)

- **GovTrack.us** — since 2004; deep data + tracking, trusted, utilitarian. No AI, no voting, no contact tools.
- **FastDemocracy** — bill tracking + alerts across all 50 states + Congress + DC + PR. State coverage is their moat.
- **5 Calls** — single-purpose: scripts to *call* reps. Best-in-class at that one thing.
- **Resistbot** — text-to-send letters to officials in <2 min.
- **POPVOX Foundation** — pivoted to government-institution tooling; no longer a consumer competitor.

---

## 2. Key findings

1. **The "AI summary + rep votes + contact" middle is now table stakes.** At least four apps shipped it in the last ~2 years. It is the price of entry, not a differentiator.

2. **Our genuine moat is three features the Tier-A clones structurally lack:**
   - **Citizen voting + aggregated community opinion** — the heart of our engagement loop; none of the near-clones have it.
   - **Per-bill discussion forums** — no community in the clone cluster.
   - **Lobbying (LDA) + donor (FEC) data tied to the specific bill** — CivicAlign has FEC for candidates; tying lobbying *firms to a bill* is rarer and more investigative.

3. **Where we're matched or behind:**
   - BillBoard's **"personal impact"** ("what this means for *you*") — we have partial (state impact); could sharpen.
   - How Congress Votes' **bill Q&A chatbot** — users increasingly expect conversational follow-up; we have static summaries.
   - FastDemocracy's **50-state coverage** — we have state bills but they lead.
   - **Retention ("why come back tomorrow")** — nobody, us included, has clearly cracked it.

---

## 3. Strategic recommendation

**Do not try to out-summarize the AI-summary crowd — that race is run.** Lean into the **community + accountability** angle the clones cannot tell:

> *The others tell you what Congress did. We let you weigh in, see where your neighbors stand, and show you who's paying for it.*

Citizen voting + discussion + money-in-politics is a combined story none of the four near-clones can match. Positioning, roadmap, and homepage copy should all push this wedge.

---

## 4. Open positioning questions (for council)

- Do we name the wedge explicitly in marketing ("the civic platform with a community, not just a feed")?
- Is the bill Q&A chatbot worth building to reach feature parity, or a distraction from the moat?
- Does "personal impact" deserve a sharpening pass given BillBoard leads there?
